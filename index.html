Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Age: Most helmets have a lifespan of about 5 to 7 years, even if they haven't been involved in a crash. Check the manufacturer's recommendation for the exact timeframe.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect the tires for cracks, flat spots, or loss of air. They might need to be inflated or replaced if they've degraded.Test the brakes to ensure they are working properly. Check the brake pads for wear and make sure they are aligned correctly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a Helmet: Always wear a properly fitting helmet to protect your head in case of a fall or collision. Obey Traffic Laws: Follow the same traffic rules as vehicles. This includes stopping at stop signs, obeying traffic signals, and yielding to pedestrians.
